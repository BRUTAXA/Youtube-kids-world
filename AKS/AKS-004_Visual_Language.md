# AKS-004 — Visual Language

**Status:** APPROVED v1.0 / Living Document  
**Phase:** Foundation → Production transition  
**Review:** ChatGPT + Claude  
**Next review:** after ART-001 and first children test

---

## 1. Philosophy

We are not designing only a style.

We are designing a memory.

The goal of each frame is that years later a person may not remember the exact plot, but remembers the feeling:

> “It was calm and warm there.”

Beauty serves the child’s emotional safety, not adult admiration.

---

## 2. Visual Safety

A frame passes only if:

- it does not feel threatening;
- it is not visually overloaded;
- it has one clear focus;
- the child understands where to look.

If at least one answer is “no”, the frame must be reworked.

---

## 3. Visual Trust

Visual Trust means the child wants to keep watching.

It is created by:

- kind eyes;
- warm eye contact;
- recognizable characters;
- recognizable Luma rituals;
- cozy textures;
- soft light;
- predictable space.

Visual Safety removes threat. Visual Trust creates attachment.

---

## 4. Shape Language

Everything friendly should be round, soft and safe.

Avoid sharp corners in friendly objects.

Roundness means safety.

---

## 5. Color Language

Use a warm, muted palette.

Avoid acidic, screaming colors.

Fixed hero colors:

- Leo — orange
- Mia — yellow
- Max — green
- Zoe — blue
- Nico — purple
- Eva — pink

Time of day may have emotional color:

- warm morning;
- soft day;
- golden evening;
- calm blue night.

Emotion through color is allowed, but only gently.

---

## 6. Light

Light should be soft, warm and golden.

Avoid harsh shadows and strong contrast.

Light carries comfort.

---

## 7. Visual Breathing

A frame must have air.

Rules:

- one clear focus;
- enough empty space;
- few moving objects;
- calm background;
- child has time to look.

> One frame — one meaning center.

---

## 8. Camera

Camera should be calm and almost invisible.

Principles:

- child eye-level;
- stable or very slow movement;
- readable wide shots;
- few cuts;
- smooth transitions;
- no shaking;
- no sudden zooms.

---

## 9. Animation

Animation should be clear, soft and readable.

Principles:

- one clear action at a time;
- calm rhythm;
- smooth beginnings and endings;
- comfortable pauses;
- no chaotic hyperactivity.

---

## 10. Character Realism

Characters are clearly stylized, not realistic.

Avoid uncanny valley.

A child should read the emotion in half a second.

---

## 11. Detail Level

AI Kids Studio uses a calm middle level of detail, slightly closer to simplicity.

Only the meaning center is detailed.

The background supports the scene but does not compete with it.

---

## 12. Frame Checklist

Before approving any frame, all answers should be “yes”:

- [ ] Is the main object clear?
- [ ] Is the frame not overloaded?
- [ ] Is the composition readable in one second?
- [ ] Does the frame feel safe?
- [ ] Are shapes rounded and friendly?
- [ ] Is the light warm and soft?
- [ ] Does it match AI Kids Studio visual DNA?
- [ ] Is it comfortable for a child, not just beautiful for an adult?

---

## Visual DNA

> **Warm rounded quietness where a child feels safe.**
