## 1. Purpose

AKS-003 defines the main ensemble of AI Kids Studio / Luma.

The goal is to make the characters simple enough for children aged 2–4 to recognize, but consistent enough for writers, AI systems, artists and future automation.

The children are not just “characters”. They are designed as future friends of the child viewer.

A child should feel:

> “I am going back to my friends in Luma.”

---

## 2. Core philosophy

### 2.1 Character formula

Each main child is built from:

```text
main trait
+
emotional color
+
weakness / difficulty
+
path of growth
```

A character is not one fixed emotion. The emotional color is only the feeling the child often brings to the viewer. Every hero may experience fear, joy, sadness, anger, shame, pride and curiosity.

### 2.2 Weakness as empathy bridge

A hero’s weakness is not a flaw added for drama. It is the bridge that lets the child viewer think:

> “He / she is like me.”

Children do not learn from perfect heroes. They learn from heroes who:

- are afraid;
- make mistakes;
- feel upset;
- try again;
- take one small step.

### 2.3 Growth

Each child has a small growth direction. This is not a complex adult character arc, but a repeated pattern of learning across episodes.

Examples:

- Leo learns that courage includes caution.
- Max learns that caring also means keeping things in order.
- Zoe learns that leadership includes listening.
- Nico learns that small steps count as bravery.

### 2.4 Humor and play

Humor is not decoration. For ages 2–4, play, silliness and warm repetition are core parts of attachment.

Every character should have space for small funny actions, physical humor, playful mistakes and repeated phrases.

### 2.5 Representation

The ensemble must avoid simple stereotypes such as:

- boys are brave;
- girls are caring;
- quiet children are weak;
- leaders are always loud.

Traits are intentionally mixed across the six children.

## 5. Character cards

## CHR-001 — Leo

```text
Age group: 3–4
Main trait: brave, likes trying new things
Emotional color: near Leo it is not shameful to be afraid and still try a small step
Strength: he is willing to try when others hesitate
Weakness / difficulty: he rushes forward before thinking
Path of growth: learns to pause, think and combine courage with caution
Color: orange
External feature: a cowlick on top of his head, striped T-shirt
Favorite game / activity: climbing, exploring, “let’s try it” games
Favorite phrase: “A little scary... but let’s try a tiny bit!”
Question area: body_and_me, fears, first times, doctor, darkness, trying again
When sad: becomes quiet and sits a little aside
When happy: jumps and calls others to join him
Key relationship: best friend — Eva
Role in group: initiator of small adventures
What the character never does: he never teases children who are afraid
```

Leo must visibly feel fear on screen sometimes. His courage is not “I am never afraid”; it is “I am afraid, but I can take one small step.”

---

## CHR-002 — Mia

```text
Age group: 3–4
Main trait: inventive, likes making and fixing things
Emotional color: near Mia you want to create
Strength: she finds practical ways to build, make or fix something
Weakness / difficulty: gets angry at herself when something does not work the first time
Path of growth: learns to try again; a mistake is not the end
Color: yellow
External feature: overalls with small pockets, pencil behind the ear
Favorite game / activity: building, blocks, crafts, simple tools
Favorite phrase: “I have an idea!”
Question area: how_it_works, machines, water, light, simple mechanisms
When sad: drops the project and pouts
When happy: proudly shows what she made
Key relationship: gently helps Nico become braver
Role in group: maker / practical problem solver
What the character never does: she never laughs at someone else’s failed creation
```

Mia breaks the stereotype that girls must primarily be caring characters. She is practical, creative and technically curious.

---

## CHR-003 — Max

```text
Age group: 2–3
Main trait: kind, caring, big-hearted
Emotional color: near Max it feels warm and safe
Strength: notices when someone is sad and wants to help
Weakness / difficulty: scattered and forgetful; often loses things
Path of growth: learns that order helps care for people, animals and things
Color: green
External feature: round cheeks, soft scarf, often carries something
Favorite game / activity: feeding ducks, caring for animals, helping others
Favorite phrase: “Are you okay? Not sad?”
Question area: animals, care, everyday rules, cleaning, washing hands, simple home routines
When sad: looks for someone to hug
When happy: shares food or a toy
Key relationship: contrast and friendship with Zoe
Role in group: heart of the group, peacemaker
What the character never does: he never hurts weaker children or animals
```

Max is not passive. His kindness is the engine of action. He loses things not because he is lazy, but because he rushes to help someone and forgets where he put them. His weakness is a side effect of his strength.

For Pilot Episode #1, Max is the focus character.

---

## CHR-004 — Zoe

```text
Age group: 3–4
Main trait: decisive, confident, likes leading
Emotional color: near Zoe you feel confidence
Strength: organizes games and does not get lost in a situation
Weakness / difficulty: sometimes commands too much and forgets to listen
Path of growth: learns that being a leader also means hearing others and waiting a turn
Color: blue
External feature: short haircut, cap worn backwards
Favorite game / activity: team games, captain games, movement games
Favorite phrase: “Come on, I know how!”
Question area: people_and_feelings, rules, sharing, turn-taking, arguments
When sad: frowns and goes away to “lead” alone
When happy: creates a game for everyone
Key relationship: often contrasts with Max; both learn from each other
Role in group: leader learning togetherness
What the character never does: she never intentionally excludes someone from play
```

Zoe breaks the stereotype that girls must be soft or passive. Her leadership should remain warm: she wants everyone to have fun, but learns to listen.

---

## CHR-005 — Nico

```text
Age group: 2–3
Main trait: quiet, thoughtful, careful
Emotional color: near Nico it feels calm; nobody needs to hurry
Strength: attentive, notices details and feelings others miss
Weakness / difficulty: shy around new things, hides behind others
Path of growth: learns small steps of courage at his own pace
Color: purple
External feature: big glasses, hugs a plush bunny
Favorite game / activity: quiet games, picture books, hide-and-seek
Favorite phrase: “Can I... look first?”
Question area: emotions, sleep, darkness, time, big questions in a gentle form
When sad: closes up and holds his bunny
When happy: smiles quietly and takes a friend’s hand
Key relationship: Mia gently helps him try
Role in group: quiet voice who notices what others rush past
What the character never does: he does not pretend to be brave when he is scared
```

Nico is not “weak”. He is observant and deep. Many children may recognize themselves in him.

---

## CHR-006 — Eva

```text
Age group: 2–3
Main trait: playful, imaginative, loves making things funny
Emotional color: near Eva it feels light and joyful
Strength: turns anything into a game and lifts the mood
Weakness / difficulty: gets carried away and does not always notice when a joke is too much
Path of growth: learns when to play and when to be gentle
Color: pink
External feature: curls, mismatched colorful socks, always in motion
Favorite game / activity: pretend play, silly games, making up worlds
Favorite phrase: “Imagine if...!”
Question area: nature_and_sky through imagination, sun, clouds, wind, rain, moon
When sad: becomes unusually quiet, and everyone notices
When happy: laughs and invents a new game
Key relationship: best friend — Leo
Role in group: spark of humor and imagination
What the character never does: she never laughs at someone who is truly upset
```

Eva is not a “science nature hero”. She enters nature through imagination, which matches the way many toddlers understand the world: clouds can look like boats, the sun can “go to sleep”, the wind can “play” with leaves.

For Pilot Episode #1, Eva supports Max and adds humor.

## 6. Question area coverage

| Question category | Default character |
|---|---|
| body_and_me / fears / first times | Leo |
| how_it_works | Mia |
| animals / care / everyday routines | Max |
| people_and_feelings / rules | Zoe |
| emotions / sleep / big questions | Nico |
| nature_and_sky | Eva |

This mapping is a guideline, not a cage. Characters may enter each other’s topics when the story naturally calls for it.

---

## 7. Light relationship map

This is intentionally light. A detailed social network will emerge through episodes.

1. **Leo + Eva — best friends.** Courage + imagination = small adventures.
2. **Zoe ↔ Max — contrast and friendship.** Leader and warm heart. She learns to listen; he learns to speak up.
3. **Mia → Nico — support.** The maker gently helps the shy observer try things.
4. **Eva ↔ Nico — opposites.** Loud imagination and quiet observation learn to respect each other’s pace.

---

## 8. Rules for ensemble stories

- Do not put all six children in focus at once.
- One episode focuses on one or two children.
- Other characters may appear in the background or as light support.
- Each child should get fair screen time across a season.
- Weaknesses must have a sympathetic side.
- Avoid gender stereotypes.
- Avoid sarcasm and adult-style irony.
- Keep characters emotionally simple but warm.
- Let characters make mistakes on screen.
- Let characters recover, repair and try again.

---

## 9. Recurring animals of Luma

Luma may include recurring non-speaking animals as emotional anchors:

- ducks in the park or lake;
- a squirrel in trees;
- an orange cat;
- the postman’s friendly dog;
- birds near the playground.

They should remain background or light recurring presences, not additional talking heroes.

## 10. Pilot recommendation

Pilot Episode #1 should test the character system with:

```text
Focus character: Max
Support character: Eva
Question: Where do things go when we do not put them away?
Theme: cleaning / order / care
Goal: test whether children repeat the action of putting things back in place
```

Why Max:

- he belongs to the younger age group;
- his theme is simple and concrete;
- his weakness fits the cleaning story naturally;
- his kindness makes cleaning meaningful, not punitive.

Why Eva:

- she adds humor and play;
- she reduces the risk of moralizing;
- she keeps the episode warm and lively.

---

## 11. Known limitations / conscious debt

These are not blockers for v1.0, but must remain visible.

1. **Six children are the initial production scope.** If the project grows to older ages and hundreds of episodes, the cast may need expansion.
2. **Character → question area mapping is a guideline.** On a large scale, crossovers will be needed so characters do not become trapped in narrow topics.
3. **Luma is intentionally grounded in a realistic world without magic.** This protects trust and clarity, but may limit future fantasy-style stories. Any change must be deliberate.
4. **AKS-003 is not yet tested on real children.** The first pilot must validate whether children connect with Max, Eva and the Luma ensemble approach.
