# DEC-Max-001 — Max Foundation v1

**Status:** APPROVED  
**Date:** 2026-06-26  
**Area:** Character Design / ART-001  
**Character:** Max  
**Children Review / OBS:** Pending

---

## Decision

Approve **Max Foundation v1** as the first official visual and emotional baseline for Max.

This is not the final eternal design.  
It is the first approved foundation for production and future children testing.

---

## Approved Direction

Max is based on **Family A** from the Character Test:

- gentle;
- calm;
- naturally caring;
- soft body language;
- helps without needing praise.

A small amount of liveliness from Family B may be used, but only if it does not change Max into an energetic/show-off character.

---

## Material Direction

Approved material direction:

> Hybrid painted material.

The material should feel warm, painted and matte — not plastic, glossy or toy-like.

---

## Core Emotion

> Helping feels natural.

Max does not help to be good.  
Max helps because he cares.

---

## Why This Was Approved

Max Foundation v1 is approved because:

- character is readable before dialogue;
- caring is expressed through posture and action;
- the “picks up the cube” action clearly communicates help without words;
- warm imperfections were added;
- the design avoids the “perfect advertising child” problem;
- the visual language supports warmth and safety;
- the character works as a production baseline for Storyboard-001.

---

## What Must Be Preserved

Future iterations must preserve:

- natural helpfulness;
- soft rounded silhouette;
- warm, painted hybrid material;
- gentle posture leaning slightly toward others;
- open hands;
- relaxed shoulders;
- small warm imperfections;
- non-show-off behavior.

---

## What Must Be Avoided

Do not turn Max into:

- a moral teacher;
- a perfect child;
- a show-off helper;
- a hyperactive comic relief character;
- a glossy plastic / toy-like design;
- a character whose effect steals focus from story.

---

## Known Open Risks

These are accepted risks until Children Review:

1. Eyes may still be slightly too large or glossy.
2. Material may still drift toward dense digital paint / light 3D.
3. Turnaround consistency must be tightened during production.
4. Children may prefer a more energetic version than adults expect.

These risks must be validated by Children Test / OBS, not by endless adult iteration.

---

## Children Review Rule

Max Foundation v1 may be changed after:

- Children Test shows weak emotional connection;
- children do not understand his helpfulness;
- children prefer another candidate clearly;
- OBS shows anxiety, confusion or lack of interest.

Until then, do not redesign Max from scratch.
