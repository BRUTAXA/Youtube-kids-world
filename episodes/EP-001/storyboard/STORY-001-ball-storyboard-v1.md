# EP-001 Storyboard v1 — «Куда спрятался мячик?»

**Episode:** EP-001 / Pilot  
**Source canon:** `STORY/STORY-001_Where_Did_The_Ball_Hide.md`  
**Canon status:** STORY-001 is approved and frozen. This storyboard adapts it; it does not rewrite it.  
**Storyboard status:** READY FOR CREATIVE REVIEW  
**Quality Gate:** Storyboard Creative Review — WAITING  
**Target age:** 2–3  
**Target duration:** ~4:55

> Production rule: this storyboard exists to get to the first animatic and Children Test. It should be readable, cheap to produce, emotionally clear, and faithful to STORY-001.

---

## Child-first storyboard intent

The child must understand the story before the words finish:

1. Max is happy with the ball.
2. The ball disappears into mess.
3. Max feels gently sad, not scared.
4. Eva turns the search into a game.
5. The room becomes clearer.
6. Max discovers the idea himself.
7. The ball comes back.
8. Max changes one tiny behavior silently.

No shot should feel like an adult cleaning lesson.

---

## Visual continuity anchors

- **Ball:** bright, round, readable in every shot where it matters. Small “boing / прыг” sound may guide attention.
- **Mess pile:** toy pile must visually hide the ball, but not feel chaotic or unsafe.
- **Toy homes:** box-with-door, shelf-nest, hook. They are visual homes, not talking characters.
- **Max:** warm, caring, slightly forgetful; never moralizing.
- **Eva:** curious, energetic, playful; her humor helps Max move.
- **Room light:** warm morning → slightly cooler during search → warm/golden after discovery.

---

## Panel list

### 1. Luma morning ritual

**Time:** 0:00–0:18  
**Shot:** wide establishing, child eye-level  
**Action:** Warm morning in Luma. Camera gently settles into Max’s room.  
**Audio:** Opening song: “Доброе утро, Лума! Как же хорошо!” Narrator: “Это Макс.”  
**Purpose:** Safety and arrival.

**Quality note:** Do not overdecorate. The room must invite the child in.

---

### 2. Max plays with the ball

**Time:** 0:18–0:35  
**Shot:** medium, low angle near floor  
**Action:** Max rolls the ball toward camera and laughs. Ball bounces softly.  
**Dialogue:** Max: “Лови! Оп! Оп!”  
**Purpose:** Establish joy and make the ball emotionally important.

**Production note:** The ball should cross the frame clearly. Children must know what is lost later.

---

### 3. Visible loss

**Time:** 0:35–0:55  
**Shot:** tracking ball, then still frame  
**Action:** Ball bounces higher, rolls under a pile of toys, and disappears. Max freezes.  
**Dialogue:** Max: “Ой...”  
**Audio:** Small signature “boing / прыг,” then room quiet.  
**Purpose:** The child sees where the problem begins.

**Quality gate check:** No narrator explanation. The loss must be visually understood.

---

### 4. Max searches alone

**Time:** 0:55–1:15  
**Shot:** medium, slightly cooler light  
**Action:** Max gently moves a few toys, looks under blocks, looks behind books.  
**Dialogue:** Max: “Где мячик? Под кубиками? Нету... За книжками? Нету...”  
**Purpose:** Let the child enter Max’s feeling before interaction begins.

**Emotion:** Sad, not panicked.

---

### 5. Eva arrives and sees the floor problem

**Time:** 1:15–1:35  
**Shot:** doorway to room, Eva enters from frame side  
**Action:** Eva runs in happily, then stops at the messy floor and tilts her head.  
**Dialogue:** Eva: “Привет, Макс! ...Ой. А где у тебя пол?”  
**Purpose:** Eva brings energy without judging Max.

**Character rule:** Eva is playful, not corrective.

---

### 6. First interaction: “Поможешь нам?”

**Time:** 1:35–1:50  
**Shot:** two-shot, then Eva looks to camera  
**Action:** Max explains. Eva gets an idea and invites the child.  
**Dialogue:** Max: “Мячик закатился. И пропал...” Eva: “А давай играть в игру «В домик»? Поможешь нам?”  
**Pause:** 3 seconds  
**Purpose:** First child participation moment.

**Quality gate check:** Pause must be real enough for a toddler to answer.

---

### 7. Toy home appears: box with a door

**Time:** 1:50–2:05  
**Shot:** close-up of box-with-door and blocks  
**Action:** Eva points to a box with a drawn door. Blocks are nearby.  
**Dialogue:** Eva: “Коробочка с дверцей любит кубики. Ням! Ням!”  
**Purpose:** Make order feel like pretend play.

**Production note:** The “home” must be simple and reusable as an asset.

---

### 8. Viewer helps: “ням в коробочку”

**Time:** 2:05–2:18  
**Shot:** Eva looking toward camera, Max holding a block  
**Action:** Eva invites viewer, Max waits with a block.  
**Dialogue:** Eva: “А ты умеешь — ням в коробочку? Покажи!”  
**Pause:** 3 seconds  
**Purpose:** Second participation moment, linked to action.

**Quality gate check:** The block must be visible during the pause.

---

### 9. Max succeeds: “В домик!”

**Time:** 2:18–2:32  
**Shot:** close-up hands/body posture, then Max face  
**Action:** Max puts blocks into the box-with-door.  
**Dialogue:** Max: “В домик!”  
**Purpose:** Give child a repeatable phrase.

**Emotion:** Small success, warm pride, no adult praise.

---

### 10. Eva’s sock joke

**Time:** 2:32–2:42  
**Shot:** medium two-shot  
**Action:** Eva puts a sock on her head. Max laughs.  
**Dialogue:** Eva: “Я носочный гномик!”  
**Purpose:** Keep story joyful; avoid cleaning-lesson feeling.

**Quality gate check:** Joke must not slow the search too much.

---

### 11. Book to shelf-nest

**Time:** 2:42–2:50  
**Shot:** shelf-nest close-up  
**Action:** Max and Eva place book onto the shelf-nest together.  
**Dialogue:** Max and Eva: “В домик!”  
**Purpose:** Repeat phrase and show room becoming clearer.

**Production note:** This can be a simple asset-reuse motion.

---

### 12. Max notices the change

**Time:** 2:50–3:10  
**Shot:** Max POV / room floor opening up  
**Action:** Last blocks move away. Max freezes. The floor and a book become visible.  
**Dialogue:** Max: “Ой... Ева, смотри! Кубики уехали в домик — и стало видно книжку! И пол!”  
**Purpose:** The discovery starts visually before Max explains it.

**Audio:** Music begins to thin out.

---

### 13. Quiet discovery

**Time:** 3:10–3:25  
**Shot:** close-up on Max face, Eva beside him  
**Action:** Max’s eyes widen. Eva watches him discover.  
**Dialogue:** Max: “Вещи не пропадают! Они прячутся в беспорядке!” Eva: “Точно! Я придумала игру, а ты придумал почему! Я и сама не знала!”  
**Audio:** Music disappears; keep soft room ambience.  
**Purpose:** Emotional and cognitive peak.

**Quality gate check:** This must feel like Max’s discovery, not narrator teaching.

---

### 14. Final search interaction

**Time:** 3:25–3:40  
**Shot:** Max looks at camera, messy area now reduced  
**Action:** Max invites viewer to look. Big slipper is visible but not too obvious.  
**Dialogue:** Max: “Значит, мячик тоже спрятался! Где? Посмотри!”  
**Pause:** 3 seconds  
**Purpose:** Third participation moment with real suspense.

**Production note:** Keep eye-line toward viewer gentle, not demanding.

---

### 15. Ball found

**Time:** 3:40–3:55  
**Shot:** close-up slipper → ball roll-out → Max reaction  
**Action:** Max lifts a big slipper. Ball rolls out.  
**Dialogue:** Max: “Мячик! Нашёлся!”  
**Audio:** Music returns only here; warm/golden light returns.  
**Purpose:** Joyful resolution.

**Quality gate check:** Ball reveal must be big and instantly readable.

---

### 16. Playing again

**Time:** 3:55–4:15  
**Shot:** medium floor play  
**Action:** Max and Eva roll the ball again and laugh.  
**Dialogue:** Eva: “Идём на улицу!”  
**Purpose:** Return to love/play, not to “cleaning completed.”

**Emotion:** Relief and shared joy.

---

### 17. Silent changed behavior

**Time:** 4:15–4:35  
**Shot:** doorway, then quiet insert of box  
**Action:** They run to the door. Max stops, silently takes the empty box and calmly puts it back in place. They run out.  
**Dialogue:** none  
**Audio:** Soft room ambience.  
**Purpose:** Show internal change without moral explanation.

**Quality gate check:** No narrator. No “good job.” The child sees the behavior.

---

### 18. Quiet room beat

**Time:** 4:35–4:40  
**Shot:** tidy room, box in place  
**Action:** Camera holds for one second of quiet.  
**Dialogue:** none  
**Purpose:** Let the emotional meaning land.

**Production note:** Not digital silence; use soft ambience.

---

### 19. Ending ritual

**Time:** 4:40–4:55  
**Shot:** Luma exterior, warm windows  
**Action:** Camera pulls back to Luma evening.  
**Dialogue:** Max and Eva: “До завтра, Лума!”  
**Purpose:** Safe closure and repeatable series rhythm.
